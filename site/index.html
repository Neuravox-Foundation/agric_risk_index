DAVARS — District Agricultural Volatility and Adaptive Risk System

Monthly agricultural risk scoring for five districts in Northern Uganda

Overview

DAVARS computes a monthly agricultural risk score (0–100) for Gulu, Arua, Lira, Oyam and Nebbi. The score combines price volatility, climate stress, production instability, conflict and disaster shocks, and input-cost pressure into a single composite index. When it exceeds a district-specific threshold, the month is flagged high-risk — a period when deploying growth capital into youth agribusinesses is statistically more likely to produce income losses.

Index Components

Data limitations. Oyam and Nebbi have no commodity price data — their DARS scores are computed from four components and flagged missing_price_component throughout. YII, ICPI and the conflict component of SII are national-level signals applied uniformly across districts. DAVARS is a prototype volatility index; it does not measure household welfare, predict specific prices, or substitute for field-level enterprise assessment.

Validation

Historical shock alignment — does DARS rise during known crisis periods?

Backtest Results

Signal lift > 1.2 means DARS was at least 20% higher on average during the documented shock than during non-shock months. Districts missing the price component are not directly comparable to full-coverage districts.

Lead–lag diagnostic: does the risk signal move ahead of price volatility?

A significant positive correlation at T+2 or T+3 indicates the composite signal tends to move 2–3 months ahead of price instability. This is a diagnostic check of co-movement, not a causal forecast.
